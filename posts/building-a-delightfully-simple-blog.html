Building a Delightfully Simple Blog

A quick walkthrough of the process behind this site.

March 18, 2024

When I decided to give my personal site a refresh, I wanted to avoid the trap of spending weeks fiddling with frameworks and build pipelines. I needed something that felt lightweight, fast to maintain, and pleasant for readers. That meant embracing good old-fashioned static HTML, a dash of CSS, and a sprinkle of progressive enhancement.

The result is what you're reading now—a handcrafted blog that renders almost instantly and is easy to publish to any static host. Here are a few guiding principles I followed:

  • Optimize for writing, not configuration.
  • Use design tokens to keep styling consistent.
  • Ship accessible, semantic markup by default.

Content structure

Posts live in individual HTML files so they can be authored in any text editor. A tiny JavaScript file powers the homepage list and formats dates, but everything still works with scripting disabled.

Simple tools are boring in the best way possible—they stay out of the way so you can focus on the work that matters.

Going forward I plan to add RSS and a projects page, but this version is intentionally minimal. Shipping now means I can iterate with real feedback instead of polishing in private.

Thanks for stopping by. If you build something similar, let me know!
