Treat Side Projects Like Tiny Labs

Exploring an experimentation mindset for creative work.

February 2, 2024

Every time I burn out on a side project it's because I tried to build a fully-fledged product in one go. The antidote has been thinking about my projects like miniature labs, each focused on a single hypothesis.

Instead of asking “how do I launch this?” I ask “what can I learn this week?” That tiny shift keeps momentum high and expectations realistic.

My favorite experiments

  1. Ship a landing page and measure who signs up for updates.
  2. Prototype the riskiest feature in a notebook or Figma.
  3. Write a blog post explaining the idea to clarify scope.

Running experiments creates feedback loops that either validate the project or nudge me toward better ideas. No experiment is a failure if it teaches me something about what to build next.

So the next time you're tempted to start another sprawling side project, give the tiny lab approach a try. You'll ship more, learn faster, and have a lot more fun along the way.
